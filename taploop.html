Building a Digital Networking Product from Hardware, Software, and Sales

TapLoop was built alongside CardMax. What started as a simple idea using NFC-enabled PVC cards became a digital networking platform with custom profiles, analytics, lead capture, corporate onboarding, and premium metal NFC products.

1,200+NFC digital business cards delivered

Beginning

Discovering NFC technology and digital business cards

TapLoop started when I discovered NFC-enabled PVC cards and saw the opportunity to connect physical networking products with digital experiences. Those first prototypes proved the concept and opened the door to a product that blended hardware, branding, and software.

First version

Building static digital business card profiles

The first commercial version of TapLoop was simple and functional: each card linked to a static digital profile through both NFC and QR. That allowed users to share their contact details instantly while I validated real market demand before investing in a larger platform.

Sales

First corporate sales meetings

Once the product had a clear value proposition, I started meeting with business clients and presenting TapLoop as a modern networking tool. Those early meetings forced me to improve my demos, learn how to communicate ROI, and handle objections from more experienced decision-makers.

Growth

Closing larger corporate deals

As confidence in the product grew, TapLoop moved from small custom runs to larger corporate orders. These deals required stronger coordination across quoting, branding, production, delivery, and onboarding, turning the project into a more structured business operation.

Platform

Transforming the product into a SaaS platform

After proving demand, I expanded TapLoop beyond static pages and turned it into a SaaS-style platform with dashboards, lead capture, and analytics. This changed the business from a one-time product sale into a more valuable and measurable software-enabled solution.

Premium

Investing in premium products and laser engraving

TapLoop kept evolving through product quality. Investing in black metal cards with laser engraving allowed me to position the brand at a more premium level and offer a product that matched higher-end professional and corporate use cases.

Scale

Scaling to 1,200+ NFC cards delivered

With better positioning, product quality, and operational discipline, TapLoop reached larger volumes and delivered more than 1,200 NFC cards. That scale represented much more than production output: it reflected product-market fit, trust from clients, and the ability to execute end to end.
